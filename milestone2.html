Milestone 2: Progress

Internal combustion engines, developed in the late 19th century, revolutionized transportation by converting fuel into mechanical energy through controlled combustion within cylinders. These engines powered automobiles, airplanes, and various other vehicles, greatly enhancing mobility and industrial progress. Their compact design, efficiency, and versatility have made them the primary propulsion system for modern transportation, shaping the world's economy and infrastructure.
